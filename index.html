About me

I am a PhD candidate in Department of Computer Science, Princeton University. My advisor is Prof. Kai Li. I am also working with Prof. Jonathan Cohen and Prof. Nicholas Turk-Browne at Princeton Neuroscience Institute. Before coming to Princeton in 2009, I received my B.E. from Northeastern University, Shenyang China, and my M.E. from Chinese Academy of Sciences, Beijing China. From 2007 to 2009, I was also a research intern student in Microsoft Research Asia.

My research interest is in computer system, high performance computing and big data analysis. Currently I am working on fMRI dataset to study whole brain scale functional connectivity (full correlation) using my own designed/developed Full Correlation Matrix Analysis (FCMA) toolbox. We have built a computer cluster for specific use of FCMA toolbox.

I used to be in web search and information retrieval field, mainly working on user generated data crawling and extraction.
